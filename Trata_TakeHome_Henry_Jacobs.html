③ Investment thesis & public writing

Thesis: Consolidators in fragmented, unglamorous industriesA consolidator is a company that rolls up many small competitors in a fragmented market — e.g., Lithia Motors buying up regional auto dealerships. — auto retail (LAD, Asbury), aerospace components (LOAR), consumer durables (Tempur Sealy). Public writing: Essentially none. Founder David Abrams keeps a famously low profile. Notable exceptions: 2019 Columbia Business School Value Investing with Legends podcast with Tano Santos, a 2017 Hedge Fund Alpha interview, occasional Barron's/Bloomberg mentions, and a few investor letters aggregated on Insider Monkey via allocator leaks.

④ Fit for Trata

✓ As a contributor
Abrams analysts are the textbook Trata contributor profile — deep, slow, expertise-driven research on a handful of names. Their take on LOAR or LAD would be high-signal for the Trata library.
✗ As a consumer
Abrams' edge is doing its own research in-house and they reportedly don't tend to buy expert-network products. ~60%+ of the book is three names held for years — they don't need idea velocity.

Best wedge if pitching: position Trata as a tool to stress-test their existing theses on names like LOAR and Asbury against analysts at peer funds — not as an idea engine.

Receptivity — mixed
$6k/year is a rounding error given their cash. But same-day idea velocity isn't a need for a long-duration value buyer.
Sale speed — slow (2–4 months+)
Private firm, centralized decision-making, decades-old research process, thorough compliance. Proof-of-value takes ~a quarter to show through their slow-turning book.

③ Investment thesis & public writing

Thesis: Nordic small/mid-cap is structurally inefficient — sell-side coverage is thin and US generalists ignore it. TIND positions itself as the institutional buyer with a real research process in that gap. Sector-agnostic, tilts toward industrials, distribution, and consumer-facing businesses. Long-biased, 15–20 high-conviction positions.

Public writing: No formal investor letters published. Hjorthen's family-office track record (since 2016, ~20.5% net annualized) is referenced in HedgeNordic but not detailed. Bjørndal is featured/quoted across HedgeNordic ("Discover the TIND Discovery Fund" May 2024, "Rookie of the Year" April 2025, "Small But Mighty: Norwegians Lead Performance Tables" Sept 2025) and Norwegian press (Finansavisen). No blog, no podcast, no Substack.

④ Fit for Trata

✓ As a consumer (conditional)
Young fund, small partnership, still proving themselves to LPs, actively looking for tools that strengthen their edge — Trata lands much better here than at an established giant. Conditional on: Trata having Nordic coverage or strong global comparables.
✓ As a contributor
Trata's analyst network is US-heavy. TIND's investment team adds rare Nordic coverage and more tickers to the library.

The hinge: Their entire edge is having sharper views on under-covered Nordic names. If Trata's library covers that universe, this is a fast yes. If not, it's a polite no.

Receptivity — depends on coverage
Yes if Trata has Nordic or comparable global coverage. No if Trata is weak there — Nordic small/mid-cap is the crux of their thesis.
Sale speed — faster than Abrams
Tiny partnership, founders sign the contract directly, no procurement layer, actively scaling. Realistic timeline: weeks, not quarters.
